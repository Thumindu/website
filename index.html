About Sri Lankan Tea

In over 150 years, the name Ceylon had become synonymous with the world’s finest tea. In the world’s eye and tongue, Ceylon was tea and tea was Ceylon. What Rolls Royce is to cars, Rolex is to watches, Havana is to cigars and Scotland is to whisky ; Ceylon is to Tea.
The legendary lion of the Sri Lankan flag was introduced to the Ceylon Tea logo - the symbol of quality.

  • 1824 – A tea plant is brought in from China by the British and planted in the Royal Botanical Gardens as a non-commercial crop.
  • 1867 – James Taylor initiates the tea industry by beginning a tea plantation (19 acres only) in the Lulkandura (Loolecondera) estate in Kandy.
  • 1899 – The area of tea plantations in Ceylon is almost 400,000 acres.

Why Sri Lankan Tea ?

An little isle situated in the Indian ocean, Sri Lanka or Ceylon is home to many wonders. Despite being a small country it is the home to the world's finest Tea.
Being an island this country is blessed with different weather conditions and elevations that allows to produce different varieties of Tea.
The use of correct planting methods, monitoring of quality control allows Sri Lanka to make the finest quality Tea!
